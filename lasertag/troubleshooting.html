Troubleshooting

Lasertag is a work-in-progress game, so it isn't a seamless experience. Meta Quest headsets are also full of quirks that can cause problems.

My headsets won't connect!

Your headsets must be on the same WiFi network. Meta headsets have a habit of disconnecting from networks when asleep or switching to other known networks in the same space.

Some WiFi networks won't work. Office and school networks often stop devices from talking to each other. You may be able to use a phone or Windows PC hotspot, but these have limited range.

My headsets won't collocate!

First, make sure they all have a proper internet connection. While Lasertag connects over LAN, collocation unfortunately relies on Meta's cloud services.

Make sure each headset gets a good look at its surroundings. If only some headsets fail, show them around the playspace more and try connecting again.

Meta's collocation system isn't the most robust. Sometimes you just need to reboot troublesome headsets. Meta service outages can also prevent collocation from working.

I get a pop-up saying 'you've moved too far!'

Meta headsets restrict movement, even in mixed reality, to a certain radius from the 'origin' (which is where you either launched the app or last re-centered). This radius is ~20 meters.

You can prevent this pop-up by disabling 'physical space features' in the settings app. Beware these resulting quirks!

  • The system boundary is dangerous disabled in all VR apps!
  • Passthrough is disabled in the system home menu.
  • Screen recordings won't capture passthrough. You'll just see a black void.

Once you are done playing Lasertag, or if you wish to capture video, make sure to turn this back on!

My video recordings of the game are black!

See above. Make sure 'physical space features' are enabled when recording video.

I can shoot through and see people's heads through walls!

If you're playing on Quest 3 or 3s, make sure you give the app 'spatial data' permission. You may have to grant it in system settings.

If you're playing on Quest 2 or Pro, these headsets unfortunately lack features necessary to understand and respond to your environment.
